JOHN
MANJIRO
1827 — 1898
Nakahama Manjiro connected Japan and America during the dawn of globalization.

After being shipwrecked as a teenager, he survived on Torishima and was rescued by an American whaling ship. He later studied navigation and Western science in America and returned to Japan during the final years of the Edo period.

His experiences became an important bridge between Japan and the wider world.
