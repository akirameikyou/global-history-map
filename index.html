MANJIRO
1827 — 1898
Nakahama Manjiro connected Japan and America during the dawn of globalization.

Shipwrecked as a teenager and rescued by an American whaling ship, he crossed the Pacific, studied navigation and Western science, and returned to Japan during the final years of the Edo period.

His life symbolized the opening of Japan to the wider world.
